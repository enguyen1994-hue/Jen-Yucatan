Yucatán

Everything you need to know before landing in Mexico's most magical peninsula. Read this once, then go live it.

México
Must-visit spots
01
Tulum Ruins
Clifftop Mayan temples overlooking turquoise water. Go at sunrise — you'll have it almost to yourself.
02
Chichén Itzá
One of the 7 Wonders of the World. Morning only — it gets brutally hot and packed by noon.
03
Cenotes
Sacred underground sinkholes full of crystal-clear water. Ik Kil and Dos Ojos are unmissable.
04
Mérida
Colonial city with world-class food, art, and nightlife. The cultural soul of the Yucatán.
05
Isla Holbox
No cars, bioluminescent water, whale sharks in summer. Pure magic. Ferry from Chiquilá.
06
Valladolid
Colorful colonial town with Cenote Zací right in the center. A perfect day trip stop.
Do this / don't do this
Do this
✓
Drink bottled or purified water only
Tap water will wreck your trip fast — even ice at sketchy spots
✓
Carry small pesos in cash
Many markets, cenotes, and taquerias are cash-only
✓
Use reef-safe sunscreen
Required at most cenotes — regular sunscreen is banned to protect the ecosystem
✓
Eat street tacos fearlessly
Find the stall with the longest line of locals — that's always the right move
✓
Download offline Google Maps
Cell signal is spotty in cenote zones and jungle roads
Don't do this
✕
Don't rent a car without budgeting for tolls
Cuotas (toll roads) add up fast — or just take ADO buses instead
✕
Don't flash expensive gear or jewelry
Petty theft is the main risk — leave valuables locked at the hotel
✕
Don't enter cenotes with sunscreen on
Shower before you swim — they'll make you anyway, but respect the ecosystem
✕
Don't skip travel insurance
Medical care isn't always cheap for tourists here — just get covered
✕
Don't wander alone at night in quiet areas
Stick to busy, well-lit areas after dark — same rules as anywhere new
Eat this
Cochinita pibil
The dish of Yucatán — slow-roasted pork in achiote paste. Get it in a taco or on a torta. Non-negotiable.
Agua de jamaica
Hibiscus flower drink, tart and sweet and cold. Order it with every single meal. You're welcome.
Sopa de lima
Tangy chicken soup with lime and crispy tortilla strips. Yucatán's soul in a bowl. Order it in Mérida.
Practical tips
💊
Pack Pepto-Bismol and Imodium
Even careful eaters can get traveler's stomach. Be prepared, not panicked.
🦟
DEET bug spray every evening
Mosquitoes carry dengue in the region — this is not optional, Jen.
🌡️
It is aggressively, relentlessly hot
80–95°F with humidity. Light linen only, refill water constantly, rest at midday.
🚌
ADO buses are your best friend
Safe, AC, wifi, and affordable. Connects Cancún, Mérida, Tulum, Playa del Carmen.
💳
USD is accepted, but pesos get better rates
Use a "casa de cambio" exchange booth — airport rates are terrible.
📱
Get a local SIM or international plan
Telcel has the best coverage across the peninsula. Sort this before you land.

Before you go
Check current travel advisories
The Yucatán is generally safe for tourists, but review state-level advisories before any road trips. Stick to main tourist corridors and you'll be absolutely fine.
Know your nearest clinic
Screenshot the nearest hospital in each city you visit. Private hospitals are faster but costlier. IMSS public hospitals exist too. Having this info is just smart.
